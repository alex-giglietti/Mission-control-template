## OVERVIEW

Partnerships let you tap into audiences that already trust someone else. Instead of building from zero, you borrow credibility and reach from established players. Affiliates are NOT referrals — they are business-to-business lead-getters who extend your marketing team, promoting your products to their audience for commissions on generated sales.

**The Partnership Equation:**
Your Offer + Their Audience + Shared Value = Accelerated Growth

**Two ways to build a compounding business:**
1. Find more people who never stop buying your stuff (Referrals/Customers)
2. Find more people who never stop selling it for you (Affiliates)

This playbook covers #2.

| Type | How It Works | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Affiliate/Referral** | They send leads, you pay commission | Ongoing lead generation at scale |
| **Joint Venture** | Co-created offer or event, split revenue | High-ticket launches, audience expansion |
| **Cross-Promotion** | Mutual promotion, no money exchanged | Similar-sized audiences, non-competing offers |
| **Podcast Guesting** | You're the expert on their show | Authority building, audience discovery |
| **Guest Content** | Blog posts, newsletter takeovers | Email list growth, SEO |
| **Bundle Deals** | Your product in their bundle | Product exposure, list building |
| **Speaking** | Present to their audience (virtual or live) | Direct selling, community access |

## HOW TO BUILD AN AFFILIATE ARMY (6-Step Process)

### Step 1: Find Your Ideal Affiliate

The ideal affiliate has a business with a warm audience full of people like your customers. Same audience, different offer — they serve who you serve but don't compete.

**Affiliate Discovery Questions (based on your ICA):**
- What do your best customers buy? → Who provides that stuff?
- Where do your best customers go? → What businesses are in those areas?
- What do your best customers like to do? → Who provides those services?
- If direct-to-consumer: the employers of your consumers could make great affiliates
- What types of businesses do they work for? What kinds of jobs do they have?

**Where to Find Partners:**

| Source | Best For |
|---|---|
| Podcasts in your niche | Guesting opportunities |
| YouTube channels | Collab videos, mentions |
| Newsletter writers | Swaps, sponsorships |
| Course creators | Affiliate, bundles |
| Community leaders | Speaking, JVs |
| Event hosts | Speaking, sponsorship |
| Complementary service providers | Referral partnerships |

**Process:**
1. List 20–50 potential partners
2. Research each: audience size/engagement, what they sell, past partnerships, contact info
3. Rank by opportunity size
4. Start outreach with top 10

### Step 2: Make the Affiliate Offer

Craft an offer that highlights the benefits FOR THE AFFILIATE — new revenue streams, added value for their audience, or both.

**The Value Equation for Affiliates:**
Make more money from your current customers and get more leads than your current offer (dream outcome)... with a high chance of working since your customers already want the product (perceived likelihood)... without needing to build, deliver, or provide customer support yourself (effort and sacrifice)... so you can start selling it tomorrow (time delay).

**5 Callout Angles for Recruiting Affiliates:**
1. Call out the affiliate business owners directly — "ATTENTION [BUSINESS TYPE] OWNERS"
2. Call out the affiliate's customers — "Do you work with [customer description]?"
3. Reference the results the affiliate's business promises — "To the heroes who [what they do]..."
4. Reference products/services the affiliates deliver — "If you sell [product type], this is for you"
5. Ask your own customers — "Do you know anyone who owns a [business type]?"

### Step 3: Qualify the Affiliates

Not everyone should be an affiliate. Qualification ensures they'll actually perform.

**Two Qualification Methods:**

**Way #1 — Make Them a Customer:**
Require affiliates to purchase and use your product first. They need to believe in it to sell it effectively, and they get the required knowledge to represent it.

**Way #2 — Make Them an Expert:**
Require affiliates to complete training or certification on your product/methodology before they can promote it. They need to know what they're talking about.

**Qualification Criteria:**
- Same audience, different offer (no direct competition)
- Good reputation (their credibility transfers to you)
- Aligned values (partnership reflects on your brand)
- Active engagement (big follower count with dead engagement = useless)
- Willing to invest time/money to understand your product

### Step 4: Figure Out What to Pay Them

Two questions to answer:
1. **What** do they get paid for? → Define the specific action you're compensating
2. **How much** do they get paid? → Calculate your maximum allowable Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Tiered Commission Structure (CAC-Based):**

| Tier | Trigger | Payout | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1: 25% CAC | Affiliate agrees to terms | Base payout | They sign up, buy product, or get certified |
| Tier 2: 50% CAC | Affiliate activates | 2x base payout | Finishes certification, makes first X posts, does first launch |
| Tier 3: 100% CAC | Affiliate sustains performance | Full payout | Maintains consistent monthly sales volume |

**Standard Commission Ranges by Offer Type:**

| Offer Type | Commission |
|---|---|
| Digital products | 30–50% |
| High-ticket programs | 10–30% |
| SaaS / subscriptions | 20–30% recurring |
| Services | 10–20% or flat fee |

**Key Metric: LTGP to CAC Ratio**
- Calculate: Lifetime Gross Profit per affiliate ÷ Cost to Acquire that affiliate
- Target: 3:1 minimum. Above 10:1 = excellent.
- Example: $54,000 LTGP ÷ $4,000 CAC = 13.5:1

**If LTGP:CAC is less than 3:1, three fixes:**
1. Lower CAC — improve ads, offer, and sales process to get affiliates for less
2. Increase LTGP & Decrease CAC — get more to activate by creating a launch process
3. Increase LTGP — make them worth more by improving integration process

### Step 5: Launch and Advertise with Affiliates

Use the **Whisper → Tease → Shout** launch method:

**Whisper:** Build curiosity with minimal details. "Something big is coming..."

**Tease:** Reveal more details, show the product, announce the launch date. Highlight key features and early results.

**Shout:** Give a clear call to action, offer bonuses, create urgency. Send frequent updates to encourage immediate sign-ups.

### Step 6: Keep Affiliates Engaged (3 Models)

**Model 1 — Affiliates Give Away Your Lead Magnet:**
The affiliate offers your free resource to their audience. Their audience gets value, you get leads, the affiliate looks good for sharing something useful. You upsell those leads into your core offer.

**Model 2 — Affiliates Sell Your Lead Magnet:**
The affiliate sells your low-ticket offer (e.g., $99 workshop). The affiliate keeps the fee (or splits it). You gain new clients who are pre-qualified buyers.

**Model 3 — Affiliates Sell Your Core Offer Directly:**
The affiliate promotes and sells your main product/program. They earn commission on each sale. This is the highest-revenue model but requires the most trust and training.

## OUTREACH SYSTEM

### Warm Before You Pitch (2–4 Weeks)
1. Follow/connect on social
2. Engage with their content (genuine comments, not "great post!")
3. Share their content
4. Build familiarity
5. Then pitch

### Outreach Templates

**Affiliate Outreach:**
> Subject: Partnership opportunity — [Your Offer]
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I love what you're doing with [their thing]. Your audience of [description] is exactly who we serve.
>
> We just launched [your offer] and I think it would be valuable for your people.
>
> Quick details: [What it is], [Who it's for], [Commission: X%], [Cookie: X days]
>
> Would you be open to a quick chat? I can share early results.
>
> [Your name]

**Joint Venture Outreach:**
> Subject: Joint workshop idea — [Topic]
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> What if we co-hosted a [workshop/webinar] on [topic]? You bring [their expertise], I bring [your expertise], we both promote, split the results.
>
> I've seen this work well — [social proof].
>
> Open to exploring?
>
> [Your name]

**Podcast Pitch:**
> Subject: Guest idea: [Specific topic angle]
>
> Hi [Host name],
>
> Just finished your episode with [guest] on [topic] — loved the point about [specific thing].
>
> I run [your company] where we help [audience] [achieve result]. Topics I could cover: [Topic 1], [Topic 2], [Topic 3].
>
> Previous appearance: [link]
>
> Would this be a fit?
>
> [Your name]
>
> P.S. Happy to promote to my audience of [size/description] as well.

**Cross-Promotion:**
> Subject: Quick promo swap?
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> We both serve [audience type]. Happy to share [your thing] with my [audience size/platform] if you'd share [their thing] with yours.
>
> Thinking a simple [email mention / social post / story share].
>
> Interested?
>
> [Your name]

**Speaking Pitch:**
> Subject: Speaker for [Event/Community Name]
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'd love to speak to your [community/event] about [topic]. I've helped [audience] achieve [results] using [your method].
>
> Happy to do [format: workshop, presentation, Q&A] and offer [their audience] [incentive].
>
> Worth discussing?
>
> [Your name]

### Follow-Up Rules
- Follow up after 5–7 days if no response
- Maximum 2–3 follow-ups
- Be gracious if they decline
- Stay connected for future opportunities

## COST & RETURNS FRAMEWORK

**Sample Calculation:**
- Cost to acquire one affiliate: $4,000 (advertising)
- Affiliate sells $10,000/month for 12 months = $120,000 total sales
- At 75% gross margin = $90,000 gross profit
- Pay affiliate 40% of gross profit = $36,000 to affiliate
- Net from that affiliate = $54,000
- LTGP:CAC ratio = $54,000 ÷ $4,000 = **13.5:1**

**Action Steps (from Multiply):**
Advertise your affiliate offer until you get 10–20 affiliates. Get results with those affiliates and use their feedback to work the kinks out of your offer, terms, launches, and integration strategy. Then scale by turning their results into your affiliate recruitment lead magnets.
