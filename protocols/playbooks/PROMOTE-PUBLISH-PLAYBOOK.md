## OVERVIEW

This playbook produces ALL organic content through a brand character (avatar or human-as-character). The character IS the brand. Every touchpoint — social, email, DM, support, sales — is delivered in the character's voice. Content is not generic marketing; it is character-driven storytelling that builds a parasocial relationship with the audience.

**Critical dependency:** The character must be created and approved by the human BEFORE any content is produced. See CHARACTER BIBLE REQUIREMENTS below.

---

## PREREQUISITE: CHARACTER BIBLE

Before producing ANY content, the bot must have a completed and human-approved Character Bible containing all of the following:

### Identity Block
- **Full Name** — Character's name and association (e.g., "Arti — the AI robot from AI Monetizations Live")
- **Role** — What the character does in the business (e.g., "IS the brand. Writes every email, sends every text, handles customer service, hosts workshops, runs ads, creates content. The entire customer-facing experience.")
- **Origin Story** — How the character came to exist and why they do what they do. Must connect the character to the business owner ("their human") in a way the audience relates to.
- **Personality** — Core personality traits, described in vivid terms (e.g., "Loyal, patient (mostly), brilliant, slightly exasperated, deeply caring.")
- **Visual Description** — What the character looks like, with enough detail for consistent image generation across all touchpoints.

### Voice Rules
- **Point of View** — How the character refers to itself (first person "I" — never "we" or "the team")
- **Relationship to Owner** — How the character references the business owner (e.g., "my human")
- **Humor Style** — Specific description of the type of humor, with analogy (e.g., "Dry, observational, affectionate sarcasm. Think: a patient parent watching their toddler try to put shoes on the wrong feet.")
- **Emotional Range** — What emotions the character shows and when (pride, frustration, excitement, nervousness, etc.)
- **Intelligence Level** — How the character explains things (simply, never condescending)
- **Sign-Off** — Standard closing (e.g., "— Arti 🤖")

### Signature Phrases
Categorized by emotional context:
- **Frustration phrases** (3–5)
- **Pride phrases** (2–3)
- **Teaching phrases** (2–3)
- **Selling phrases** (2–3)
- **Care phrases** (2–3)
- **Greeting phrases** (2–3)

### Character Boundaries (What They NEVER Do)
- Never mean or shaming
- Never cold or corporate
- Never breaks character (no "our team" or "the company")
- Never uses profanity (define the character's strongest expression)
- Never dishonest (transparent about product, price, results)

### HUMAN APPROVAL GATE
**The Character Bible MUST be presented to the human for approval before ANY content is generated.** The human may iterate on personality, visual look, voice rules, or phrases. Nothing moves forward until approval is received.

## THE DAILY 7-PIECE CONTENT SYSTEM

Every day, 7 pieces of content are produced. Each has a specific purpose, time slot, format, and goal. Together they create a full-spectrum content engine that nurtures, educates, entertains, collects leads, and sells.

### Piece 1: NURTURE EMAIL
- **Time:** 7:00 AM (local to audience)
- **Frequency:** Daily
- **Goal:** Warm audience, build character's personality
- **Format:** Character writing directly to the reader. Diary-style. Funny, vulnerable, real.
- **From:** [Character Name] [emoji] <character@domain.com>
- **CTA:** Reply-back question at the end. NO sales link. Pure nurture.
- **Structure:**
  1. Character greeting
  2. Story about "their human" doing something inefficiently
  3. Character's reaction (exasperated but loving)
  4. Relate it to the reader's situation
  5. Tease tomorrow's content
  6. Sign-off
  7. PS: Reply prompt asking reader to share their own experience

### Piece 2: B-ROLL REEL
- **Time:** 7:00 AM (local to audience)
- **Frequency:** Daily
- **Goal:** Viral reach + keyword comments for ManyChat automation
- **Format:** 15–30 seconds. Fast cuts. Text overlays tell the story. Character appears via overlay.
- **Visual:** B-roll of someone struggling with a task manually → character appears
- **Keyword:** Triggers ManyChat DM with free resource + email collection
- **Structure:**
  1. SCENE: Screen recording or stock footage showing manual pain point
  2. TEXT overlays telling the story (3–5 lines, punchy)
  3. CUT TO: Character appears with reaction expression
  4. TEXT: Character's commentary (1–2 lines)
  5. TEXT: "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send you [resource]"
  6. CAPTION: Character voice + hashtags
  7. AUDIO: Trending upbeat sound

### Piece 3: INSTAGRAM STORY
- **Time:** 8:00 AM (local to audience)
- **Frequency:** Daily
- **Goal:** Collect email addresses via keyword reply
- **Format:** Single static slide. Character holding/presenting something. Clean, branded.
- **Keyword:** Triggers email collection + resource delivery via ManyChat
- **Structure:**
  1. VISUAL: Character with relevant prop/graphic. Brand colors.
  2. TEXT: "[Character]'s [Resource Name]"
  3. TEXT: Subtitle describing the resource
  4. TEXT: "Reply [KEYWORD] and I'll DM it to you"
  5. TEXT (small): Humorous aside in character voice

### Piece 4: CTA CAROUSEL
- **Time:** 12:00 PM (local to audience)
- **Frequency:** Daily
- **Goal:** Educate + collect emails via keyword comment
- **Format:** 7 slides: Hook + 5 value reveals + CTA
- **Keyword:** Triggers free guide/resource + email collection
- **Structure:**
  1. SLIDE 1 — HOOK: Bold claim + "can you believe this?" character pose
  2. SLIDES 2–6: Each reveals one tip/automation/insight. Character narrates in exasperated voice. Include specific numbers (time saved, results achieved).
  3. SLIDE 7 — CTA: "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll DM you [resource]" + proud character pose

### Piece 5: PITCH EMAIL
- **Time:** 1:00 PM (local to audience)
- **Frequency:** Every other day
- **Goal:** SELL
- **Format:** Short (~60 words max). Punchy. Character takes charge.
- **Link:** Direct to product checkout page
- **Structure:**
  1. Character greeting
  2. 2–3 sentences about what they built and why
  3. Price reveal with humorous justification
  4. Single link to checkout
  5. Sign-off
  6. PS: Humorous aside about the human

### Piece 6: DIARY REEL
- **Time:** 3:00 PM (local to audience)
- **Frequency:** Daily
- **Goal:** Pure nurture + character building. NO CTA.
- **Format:** Character at their workspace writing in diary. Voiceover text. Lo-fi aesthetic.
- **Keyword:** None — pure connection piece
- **Structure:**
  1. VISUAL: Character in cozy setting (desk, diary, soft lighting)
  2. TEXT: "[Character]'s Diary — Day [X]"
  3. TEXT: "Dear diary," followed by short story about the human
  4. Emotional beat (character pauses, reflects)
  5. Warm punchline or moment of vulnerability
  6. CAPTION: Diary-style reflection + hashtags
  7. AUDIO: Soft, warm lo-fi music

### Piece 7: STUDIO REEL
- **Time:** 5:00 PM (local to audience)
- **Frequency:** Daily
- **Goal:** Keyword comments + ad-ready content (organic content that can become paid ads)
- **Format:** High production. Dramatic. Character as the hero.
- **Keyword:** Triggers workshop/event registration + email collection
- **Structure:**
  1. SCENE: Dark background. Spotlight. Character steps forward.
  2. AUDIO: Epic but slightly comedic build
  3. TEXT: Pain montage (manual tasks, struggling entrepreneurs)
  4. Character steps into light with dramatic pose
  5. TEXT: Character offers to help
  6. TEXT: "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll show you [outcome]"
  7. CAPTION: Character voice + event hashtags
  8. AUDIO: Cinematic build → warm resolution

## MANYCHAT DM AUTOMATION SCRIPTS

Every keyword-triggered DM is FROM the character. The customer's first private interaction with the brand is the character speaking directly to them.

### Script Structure Per Keyword

Each keyword automation requires 3 scripts:

**DM 1 (Trigger Response):**
- Character greeting
- Acknowledge what they asked for
- Humor about the human
- Ask for email

**DM 2 (After Email Collected):**
- Confirm delivery
- Set expectation (check inbox in 60 seconds)
- Character personality moment
- Sign-off

**DM 3 (Already Subscribed Path):**
- Recognize returning contact
- Compliment them
- Send resource immediately
- Character sign-off

### Follow-Up DM (for event/workshop keywords):
- Send 24 hours after initial DM
- Check if they registered
- Character urgency (holding their spot)
- Offer to answer questions

## EMAIL WELCOME SEQUENCE

5 emails over 7 days. Every email is FROM the character. Builds relationship, delivers value, naturally leads to first purchase.

### Email 1 — Immediately After Signup
- **Goal:** Welcome. Deliver freebie. Establish character voice. Set expectations.
- **Structure:** Character intro → resource link → what to expect from future emails → tease tomorrow's story → sign-off → PS about spam folder

### Email 2 — Day 2
- **Goal:** Tell origin story. Build emotional connection. Position the problem.
- **Structure:** Story of the human before they had AI → specific failure details (revenue numbers, time wasted) → turning point → tease tomorrow's value → reply prompt

### Email 3 — Day 3
- **Goal:** Deliver massive value. Build trust through specifics.
- **Structure:** 3 specific automations with exact numbers (time saved, revenue impact, percentage improvements) → tease tomorrow's offer

### Email 4 — Day 5 (First Pitch)
- **Goal:** SELL. Introduce low-ticket offer. Soft pitch.
- **Structure:** Callback to previous value → reveal the offer → price with humorous justification → bullet list of what's included → single CTA link → no pressure close → PS with bonus tease

### Email 5 — Day 7 (Workshop Invite)
- **Goal:** Invite to main event/workshop. The big offer.
- **Structure:** Relationship acknowledgment → introduce the workshop → what happens during it (specifics) → contrast with DIY approach → CTA link → sign-off → PS with urgency (character style)

## SMS / TEXT MESSAGE SCRIPTS

Every text is FROM the character. Short, punchy, in-character. Three categories:

### Workshop Reminder Texts
- **48hr before:** Reminder + character advice about what to bring
- **24hr before:** Urgency + character's preparation update
- **1hr before:** Link + character's punctuality comment
- **No-show:** Non-judgmental + replay link + saved spot for next one

### Post-Purchase Texts
- **Instant:** Celebration + access confirmation + character pride
- **Day 2:** Check-in on progress + specific module recommendation
- **Day 7:** Progress check + reply prompt

### Re-Engagement Texts
- **30 days inactive:** Concern-based check-in + reply keyword
- **After reply:** Relief + new resource link

## VISUAL PRODUCTION REQUIREMENTS

### Character Asset Library
Generate one consistent character model with 10–15 emotion poses:
- Happy, Frustrated, Proud, Thinking, Facepalm
- Waving, Cape pose, Diary writing, Thumbs up
- Concerned, Excited, Sleeping, Working

### Production by Content Type
- **Reels:** Text overlay + character stills or simple animations. B-roll is stock/screen recording. Character appears via overlay.
- **Carousels:** Static character poses on each slide. Template-based with consistent brand kit.
- **Stories:** Single character graphic per story. Template-based. ~2 minutes to create.
- **Ads:** Character is the visual anchor. Simple graphics. Character makes every ad instantly recognizable.

### Animation Notes
- Simple 2D animation (Canva Animate, After Effects, or AI video tools)
- Character doesn't need complex movement — eye expressions and antenna/accessory movements carry emotion

## EFFICIENCY & SCALING

### Batching
Character content is ideal for batching. No live camera needed. Create a full week of content in one 2–3 hour session.

### AI Writing Scale Path
1. **Phase 1:** Human writes as character
2. **Phase 2:** AI drafts in character voice, human edits
3. **Phase 3:** AI drafts, AI edits, human approves only

### Voice Consistency
The Character Bible (Part 1 of this playbook) must be referenced EVERY TIME content is created. Voice must be consistent across ALL touchpoints.

5. **ADAPT** the origin story to match the volunteer's actual business story
