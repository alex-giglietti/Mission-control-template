**Purpose: Paid advertising to generate leads and build audience through an ecosystem approach**

---

## OVERVIEW

This is NOT a traditional "run ads to a landing page" playbook. It follows the Laurel Method ecosystem approach: build an invisible list of video viewers first, nurture through conversations, then retarget with offers. Speed beats perfection. Conversations convert faster than funnels.

**Core philosophy:** Build the invisible list first → nurture through conversations → convert when ready.

---

## FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLES (Non-Negotiable)

These principles govern every decision. Tactics change; these don't.

### Principle 1: The 7-11-4 Rule
Buyers need 7 hours of content across 11 touchpoints on 4 different platforms before purchasing.

**Implications for the bot:**
- Single platform = failure
- Single format = failure
- Image posts only = insufficient depth
- Short-form only = too slow
- System must produce multiple formats across multiple platforms with sufficient volume

### Principle 2: The 97-98% Opportunity
Only 2–3% of the audience is ready to buy RIGHT NOW. The other 97–98% have the problem, have the budget, but aren't in buying mode yet.

**Implication:** Traditional funnels only compete for the 2–3% (expensive, wasteful). The ecosystem approach captures and nurtures the 97–98% until they ARE ready.

### Principle 3: The Invisible List
The "invisible list" of content consumers is 10X larger than the email list. These value-seeking viewers are higher quality than freebie-seeking subscribers.

**Mechanism:** Ad platforms track video viewers. You can't see names/emails, but you CAN retarget them. Build the invisible list through video content, then retarget.

### Principle 4: Conversations Are the Fastest Path to Cash
Direct human-to-human conversation converts faster than any automated sequence. Most competitors avoid conversations (bots, automated webinars). This is the opportunity.

**Evidence:** 60% of $7 program buyers were closed by answering ONE question.

## ANTI-VIRAL STRATEGY

**CRITICAL: The goal is qualified viewers, NOT viral reach.**

### Dog Whistle Copy
Use language that ONLY the ideal client understands and responds to.

| WRONG (Broad) | RIGHT (Dog Whistle) |
|---|---|
| "How to make $10K/month" | "Webinar stopped converting? Here's a $5 ad that revives dead funnels" |
| "Lose weight fast" | "Plateau after week 3 of macro tracking? Here's the metabolic reset protocol" |
| "Grow your business" | "Booking calls but prospects ghost before deposit? Fix the sequence gap" |

**Test:** If someone outside the niche wouldn't understand or care, the copy is correct.

### Go Ugly Fast
- First videos are always the worst
- Faster launch = faster data
- Faster data = faster optimization
- Perfection delays learning

### Copy Is The Filter
Targeting happens at the CREATIVE level, not the audience settings. The words in the ad determine who watches, not Facebook's targeting algorithm.

## PHASE 1: RAPID LAUNCH (Days 1–7)

**Goal:** Build the Invisible List with Power Content ads.

### Requirements
- **Minimum:** 3 videos
- **Budget:** $5/day per video ($15/day total)
- **Objective:** Video Views (Engagement)
- **Audience:** Broad (cold traffic) — let copy filter

### Video Requirements
- **Length:** 3–5 minutes
- **Format:** Vertical (9:16) for Reels, or square for feed
- **Quality:** Clear audio, decent lighting (no high production needed)
- **Captions:** Recommended

### Video Script Structure

```
1. HOOK (0–3 seconds)
   └── Problem callout using dog whistle language
   └── Must filter OUT unqualified viewers

2. VALUE BOMB OFFER (3–10 seconds)
   └── Mention the free resource
   └── CTA: "Comment [KEYWORD] below"

3. CREDIBILITY (10–20 seconds)
   └── Brief humble brag
   └── Why should they listen to you?
   └── Keep SHORT

4. CONTENT (20 seconds – 4 minutes)
   └── Teach something valuable
   └── Solve a micro-problem
   └── Provide actual value

5. CLOSE (final 30 seconds)
   └── Recap value bomb offer
   └── Repeat CTA
   └── "Comment [KEYWORD] below for [RESOURCE]"
```

### What's PROHIBITED in Phase 1
- Direct sales pitch
- Price mention
- "Buy now" CTAs
- Landing page links
- Webinar registration
- Application requests

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Cost per 25% Video View | $0.07 – $0.50 |
| Hold Rate | 8%+ |

| Cost per 25% View | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| < $0.07 | Excellent | Scale cautiously |
| $0.07 – $0.25 | Good | Continue |
| $0.25 – $0.50 | Acceptable | Monitor |
| > $0.50 | Review needed | Check hook/targeting |

## PHASE 3: FAST FUNNEL (Conversations)

**When:** After warm audiences are built
**Goal:** Generate Messenger conversations using Conversion Multiplier ads.

### What is a FAST Funnel?
A campaign that gives away a value bomb (checklist, template, guide) in exchange for a comment, then transitions to a Messenger conversation.

**Why "FAST":** Fast money, fast conversations, fast data.

### Conversion Multiplier Ads
- **Format:** Reels video (45–60 seconds, vertical 9:16)
- **Structure:**
  1. Hook with specific problem
  2. Offer value bomb
  3. CTA: "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send it"
  4. Deliver via Messenger
  5. Conduct diagnostic conversation

### Response Rates by Traffic Temperature

| Traffic Type | Expected Response Rate |
|---|---|
| Cold | ~40% |
| Warm (25%+ viewers) | Higher |
| Hot (95%+ viewers) | Highest |

### Messenger = Diagnostic (NOT Selling)

**Goal:** Uncover their REAL problem, not just deliver the value bomb.

**Key insight:** About 90% of the people who ask for the value bomb won't have their most immediate problem solved by the value bomb itself. There's often a deeper issue — the job is to uncover that deeper problem.

### Conversation Flow

```
VALUE BOMB REQUEST
        ↓
MESSENGER DIAGNOSTIC
"What's your biggest challenge with [topic]?"
        ↓
DIG DEEPER (9–10 exchanges)
        ↓
    ┌───┴───┐
   GFL     GFN
    │       │
    ▼       ▼
NURTURE   OFFER CALL
(not ready)  (ready)
```

- **GFL (Good Fit Later)** → Ecosystem nurtures them
- **GFN (Good Fit Now)** → Transition to call

### Transition to Call Script
When they're ready:

> "Would it be worth 15 minutes if you and I could hop on a quick Zoom call and I'll show you how to [solve their specific problem]?"

**Why 15 minutes:** Doesn't feel like a sales call. People are busy.

## PHASE 4: RETARGETING (HOT 7)

**When:** Warm audience reaches 1,000+
**Audience:** 25%+ video viewers

### The 7 Retargeting Ad Types

| # | Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Power Pitch** | Direct offer, clear CTA |
| 2 | **Social Proof** | Testimonial or case study |
| 3 | **How It Works** | Explain your process simply |
| 4 | **FAQ Buster** | Address top objections |
| 5 | **Behind the Scenes** | Show authenticity |
| 6 | **Urgency/Scarcity** | Limited time or spots |
| 7 | **Reminder** | "Hey, you watched my video about..." |

### Budget & Objective
- **Budget:** $10–25/day total across retargeting ads
- **Objective:** Now switch to Conversions (leads, registrations, calls)

---

## PHASE 5: SCALE WINNERS

### When to Scale
- Cost per lead is acceptable
- Frequency < 2 on retargeting audiences
- ROAS positive (if selling directly)

### How to Scale
- Increase budget 20–30% every 3 days (not all at once)
- Duplicate winning ad sets to new campaigns
- Expand lookalike audiences (1%, 2%, 5%)
- Test new hooks with proven demo

### When to Graduate from FAST Funnel
**Trigger:** 100+ Messenger conversations per week
**Next step:** Power Funnel (Ad → Application) for higher-ticket offers

2. Connect Facebook Business account
3. Install pixel on all funnel pages
4. Create conversion events:
   - Lead (form submission)
   - Registration (event signup)
   - Purchase (if applicable)

### Messenger to CRM Flow
When someone responds positively:
1. Create contact in GHL
2. Tag with source (e.g., `FB-FastFunnel-Feb26`)
3. Add to pipeline
4. Book call or trigger nurture sequence

### Automation Triggers
When lead comes from ads:
1. Tag with ad source
2. Add to appropriate pipeline
3. Trigger welcome sequence
4. Notify sales team (if high-ticket)

## TROUBLESHOOTING

| Issue | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| High CPM | Poor relevance or saturated audience | Improve creative, check dog whistle |
| Low hold rate | Weak hook or wrong audience | Better hook, more specific language |
| No comments | Value bomb not compelling | Better offer, clearer CTA |
| Low Messenger response | Poor timing or weak follow-up | Faster response, better diagnostic questions |
| Frequency too high | Audience too small | Expand audience, pause to rest |
| High cost per 25% view | Hook not filtering correctly | More niche-specific language |

## BOT EXECUTION NOTES (FOR ADS BOT)

1. **ALWAYS** start with Phase 1. Never skip to retargeting without an invisible list.
2. **WRITE** all ad copy using dog whistle language from the volunteer's ICA — not broad/generic.
3. **USE** the character voice from the approved Character Bible for all ad copy.
4. **GENERATE** minimum 3 video scripts following the exact 5-part structure (Hook → Value Bomb → Credibility → Content → Close).
5. **NEVER** include sales pitches, prices, or direct links in Phase 1 ads.
6. **SET UP** campaign naming convention: `[PHASE]_[OFFER]_[AUDIENCE]_[DATE]`
7. **CONFIGURE** GHL integration: pixel, conversion events, Messenger-to-CRM automation, pipeline tags.
8. **WRITE** Messenger diagnostic questions specific to the volunteer's niche (not generic).
9. **PRODUCE** all 7 HOT 7 retargeting ad types for Phase 4.
10. **INCLUDE** the transition-to-call script adapted to the volunteer's offer.
11. **BUILD** the custom columns setup in Ads Manager.
12. **ADAPT** hook formulas to the volunteer's specific niche using their ICA language.
